Waste Segregation

Separate waste into biodegradable (green), recyclable (blue), and hazardous (red) categories.

  • Organic waste: food scraps, garden waste
  • Recyclables: paper, plastic, glass, metal
  • Hazardous: batteries, electronics, medical waste

Composting at Home

Turn your kitchen waste into nutrient-rich compost for plants.

  • Use a composting bin or pit
  • Add vegetable peels, fruit waste, tea bags
  • Avoid meat, dairy, and oily foods
  • Mix regularly and keep moist

Reduce Plastic Usage

Minimize single-use plastics to reduce environmental impact.

  • Carry reusable bags and water bottles
  • Avoid plastic straws and cutlery
  • Choose products with minimal packaging
  • Recycle plastic properly

Hazardous Waste Disposal

Dispose of dangerous materials safely to protect health and environment.

  • Never mix with regular waste
  • Use designated collection centers
  • Handle with care (batteries, chemicals)
  • Follow local disposal guidelines
